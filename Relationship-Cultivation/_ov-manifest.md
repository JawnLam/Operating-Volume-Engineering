# Relationship-Cultivation — Fresh Worked Example (Lighter Depth)

> **A lighter worked example for the OV form applied to ongoing personal relationships — a "relational CRM" for people the user cares about (close friends, mentors, family, important professional relationships). Demonstrates that the OV form works for low-frequency, long-horizon, relational domains, not just episodic or decisional ones.**

## What this OV would be

An OV for **cultivating personal relationships intentionally over years**: keeping track of context, last touches, things-said, gift ideas, conversation threads, and the rhythm of contact across a network of people who matter.

The cartridge analog is **a person** (one cartridge per important relationship), and the engagement is the ongoing maintenance of that relationship across years.

This is the most cross-cartridge-heavy OV of the three fresh examples — there's a *network view* that matters more than any single cartridge. The OV must support both per-person depth and across-network awareness.

## Why this user would design this

Important relationships erode silently if not tended. Birthdays slip; significant moments go un-acknowledged; the user forgets what their friend's daughter is doing in college this semester. A serious adult with a wide network of consequential relationships needs **infrastructure** — and an OV is exactly the substrate-agnostic infrastructure that fits.

This is **not** for casual acquaintances or work-only contacts; tools like LinkedIn or HubSpot already handle those. It's for the 15–50 people who genuinely matter and whom the user wants to be in active, intentional contact with.

## Who is this for

OVE worked example — illustrative.

## Domain shape

- **Relational** (primary)
- **Operational** (high) — scheduling, follow-up, touch logging
- **Declarative/factual** (medium) — who, what, when
- **Decisional** (low) — occasionally (a gift choice, a difficult conversation)
- Minimal: formal, procedural, creative, experiential

## Cadence

**Continuous and low-frequency.** Touches happen as they happen — a text, a call, a meal, a card. The OV doesn't drive a regular practice rhythm; it captures the rhythm that exists and surfaces gaps.

This is structurally different from all prior OVs. It's neither episodic-on-event (Negotiation), daily-practice (Writing), nor periodic study (LLL). It's **ambient + nudge-driven** — the AI surfaces things when relevant.

## Multi-session evidence

Strong but unusual:

- Each cartridge (person) is long-lived (years or lifetimes)
- Session frequency per cartridge is low (perhaps once a month or quarter)
- Session frequency across the OV is moderate (daily or weekly across the whole network)

## Output target

Not a deliverable in the SOLVE-eX or Negotiation-Prep sense. The output is **a richer relational life**: more thoughtful touches, fewer missed moments, deeper conversations because the user remembers what was said last time, intentional cultivation of relationships that would otherwise drift.

Optional artifacts: annual relationship audit, gift-list export, "people I haven't been in touch with in N months" report.

## Sensitivity and ethics

High. The OV is, by design, surveillance-adjacent. The user is recording personal information about people who haven't consented to being in a database. Guardrails:

- **For the user's eyes only.** The cartridges are never shared without the relationship-other's consent.
- **Never used to manipulate.** The OV refuses to design persuasion or manipulation toward the people in it.
- **Recorded information stays factual.** Not "Marcus is insecure"; instead "Marcus mentioned his manager review went poorly."
- **Hard line on therapy-adjacent content.** If the cartridge starts containing what the user wants to *do to* the other person rather than what they want to *do with* the other person, route to a therapist.

## Notes for OV-design students

This worked example demonstrates **the most unusual cartridge shape of the three fresh examples**:

1. **Cartridge = a person** (a fixed entity, not an event or a project)
2. **Cross-cartridge view matters more than single cartridge** — the network is a first-class concern
3. **Cadence is ambient + nudge-driven** rather than rhythmic
4. **Ethics are foreground**, not background — the schema must include explicit constraints on what's recorded and what the OV refuses to do
5. **The "deliverable" is a richer life**, not a document — the OV ships meaning rather than artifacts

A complete shipping of this OV would need 6–10 more design sessions.
