## § 1 — Personal Evaluation (14-day window)

A reader may, for personal evaluation purposes, read and consider the OV's published content for a period of **fourteen (14) calendar days** beginning at first download or first read, subject to all six (6) limits below:

1. **Application-prohibited.** Evaluation does not include application of the OV's methodology to any active engagement, sphere, client situation, or principal-self-coaching context. Evaluation is *reading and considering*, not *using*.
2. **No-sphere-creation.** Evaluation does not include the creation of any sphere cartridge, engagement cartridge, or other OV cartridge structure populated with the evaluator's own situation. The OV's templates may be reviewed structurally but not instantiated with real or representative content.
3. **No-reference-during-engagement.** During any active client engagement, advisory conversation, or principal-self-coaching context that the evaluator is conducting independently of this evaluation, the evaluator may not reference the OV's methodology, vocabulary, or frameworks. The OV's substrate cannot be "informally absorbed" during evaluation and then reproduced in paid engagements without an explicit licensing arrangement.
4. **No-retention-after-expiration.** At the end of the 14-day evaluation window, the evaluator must delete or destroy all copies of the OV's content in their possession, subject to § 4 (Academic Archive Carve-Out).
5. **Burden-of-distinction.** The evaluator bears the burden of demonstrating, if questioned, that any subsequent practice or methodology in their own work was developed independently of the OV's substrate. The evaluation period does not confer any presumption of independent development.
6. **No-derivative-works.** Evaluation does not include the creation of summaries, derivative methodologies, "inspired-by" frameworks, or any other work product that incorporates, restates, or substantially mirrors the OV's substrate.

## § 2 — Principal Self-Coaching (NOT personal evaluation)

A principal who is the Methodology Author themselves, or a principal who has received explicit written authorization from the Methodology Author, may use the OV for self-coaching in their own active engagements. This use **is NOT personal evaluation under § 1 under any circumstance** — the 14-day window, application-prohibited limit, and no-sphere-creation limit do not apply to authorized self-coaching by an authorized principal.

The intent of § 2 is to close the loophole where a principal might claim "personal use by a principal coaching themselves" is personal evaluation. It is not. Self-coaching by an authorized principal is **licensed application**, not evaluation, and is governed by the terms of the principal's authorization (typically a separate written agreement).

Self-coaching by an *unauthorized* principal — a principal who has not received explicit written authorization from the Methodology Author — is not licensed under this LICENSE and is governed by § 1's evaluation terms (with the application-prohibited limit applying in full).

## § 3 — Commercial Use, Redistribution, Derivative Works

Commercial use, redistribution, and derivative works require **explicit written authorization** from the Methodology Author. No default license is granted. Examples requiring authorization:

- Redistributing the OV's content in whole or in part (including via fork, mirror, training data, or any other channel)
- Creating derivative methodologies that incorporate the OV's substrate
- Incorporating the OV's content into any commercial product or service

Authorization is granted case-by-case. Request via `<Methodology Author Contact>`.

## § 4 — Academic Archive Carve-Out

If, during the 14-day evaluation period, an evaluator legitimately archives portions of the OV's content **for completed or submitted scholarly research** — e.g., as cited material in a peer-reviewed paper, a thesis, a dissertation, or a published book — that archive may be preserved beyond the 14-day window, subject to four conditions:

1. **Time-bound to completed/submitted research.** The carve-out applies only to research already completed or submitted at the time of evaluation-window expiration. It does not extend to "future research I might do."
2. **Archival-use-only.** The archived material may be cited and referenced within the scholarly research but may not be re-used in unrelated work or redistributed.
3. **Notification-on-request.** The evaluator notifies the Methodology Author, on request, of the specific archived materials and the scholarly research they support. The notification is informational, not authorization-seeking.
4. **Lapses on research withdrawal.** If the cited scholarly research is later withdrawn, retracted, or never published, the archive obligation reverts to § 1.4 (delete or destroy).

The carve-out exists because academic fair-use citation is structurally incompatible with delete-or-destroy obligations — a scholar who cited the OV's substrate in a published paper would otherwise face an irresoluble conflict between this LICENSE and their academic integrity obligations.
