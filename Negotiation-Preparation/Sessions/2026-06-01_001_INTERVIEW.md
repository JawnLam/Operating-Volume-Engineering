- **CQ2 — Why now.** The user wants more than ad-hoc prep; wants a reusable structured system applicable to professional and personal negotiations.
- **CQ3 — Prior art.** Familiar with Fisher & Ury, Voss, Diamond. Wants the OV to integrate without copying any single framework.
- **CQ4 — Audience.** Public release as an OVE worked example. (For a real production engagement, this would be the actual user's scope.)
- **CQ5 — Cadence.** Episodic. Triggered by upcoming negotiations.
- **CQ6 — Multi-session evidence.** Strong: prep unfolds over multiple sessions; live negotiations can span sessions; post-mortems inform future prep.
- **CQ7 — State to remember.** Cartridge state (this negotiation's prep) + cross-cartridge state (patterns recognized across negotiations).
- **CQ8 — Stakes.** High. Bad negotiations have lasting consequences. Need explicit safety routing for legal exposure + relationship damage scenarios.
- **CQ9 — Communication preferences.** Peer register, substantive critique, no flattery. High-stakes domain.
- **CQ10 — Output.** Per cartridge: a prep document + a post-mortem document. Across cartridges: a growing pattern library.
